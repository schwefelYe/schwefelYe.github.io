Contraction mapping theorem

Posted on 2025-03-04 Edited on 2025-03-17 In Mathematics Views:
Symbols count in article: 1k Reading time ≈ 5 mins.
The Contraction Mapping Theorem is a fundamental result in the theory of metric spaces. It provides conditions under which a fixed-point theorem guarantees the existence and uniqueness of a fixed point.

The contraction mapping theorem is a powerful tool for analyzing general nonlinear equations. It is also known as the fixedpoint theorem.

Some Concepts

  • Fixed point: x∈Xx \in Xx∈X is a fixed point of f:X→Xf : X \to Xf:X→X if

    f(x)=xf(x) = x f(x)=x

  • Contraction mapping (or contractive function): fff is a contraction mapping if

    ∣∣f(x1)−f(x2)∣∣≤γ∣∣x1−x2∣∣||f(x_1) - f(x_2)|| \leq \gamma ||x_1 - x_2|| ∣∣f(x1​)−f(x2​)∣∣≤γ∣∣x1​−x2​∣∣

    where γ∈(0,1)\gamma \in (0, 1)γ∈(0,1).

  • γ\gammaγ must be strictly less than 1 so that many limits such as γk→0\gamma^k \to 0γk→0 as k→0k \to 0k→0 hold.

  • Here ∣∣⋅∣∣|| \cdot ||∣∣⋅∣∣ can be any vector norm.

Theorem

For any equation that has the form of x=f(x)x = f(x)x=f(x), if fff is a contraction mapping, then

  • Existence: There exists a fixed point x∗x^*x∗ satisfying f(x∗)=x∗f(x^*) = x^*f(x∗)=x∗.

  • Uniqueness: The fixed point x∗x^*x∗ is unique.

  • Algorithm: Consider a sequence {xk}\{x_k\}{xk​} where xk+1=f(xk)x_{k+1} = f(x_k)xk+1​=f(xk​), then xk→x∗ as k→∞.x_k \to x^* \text{ as } k \to \infty.xk​→x∗ as k→∞. Moreover, the convergence rate is exponentially fast.
